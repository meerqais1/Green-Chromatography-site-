Comparative Evaluation of Green Chromatographic Techniques in HPLC and GLC for Regulatory Applications in the Pharmaceutical and Food Industries

Introduction

This research explores eco-friendly chromatographic techniques in HPLC and GLC to support pharmaceutical and food industry regulations, focusing on minimizing environmental impact without sacrificing precision.

Problem Statement

There is limited comparative research on green HPLC vs. green GLC within regulatory settings. This research addresses industry uncertainty and regulatory gaps.

Objectives

  • Compare performance, efficiency, and compliance of green HPLC and GLC.
  • Identify current green practices and regulatory influences.
  • Highlight industrial case studies.

Research Questions

  • What are the recent developments in green chromatography?
  • How do green HPLC and GLC compare in accuracy and sustainability?
  • What are the barriers and benefits of green chromatography adoption?

Methodology

A mixed-methods approach combining analytical metrics, case studies, and regulatory document analysis, using tools like SWOT and trend graphs.

Expected Outcomes

  • Empirical data supporting green method adoption.
  • Guidelines for industry transitions to greener approaches.
  • Recommendations for regulatory bodies.
